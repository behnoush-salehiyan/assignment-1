Vitreous enamel Minakari is an art with a history of about five thousand years, used to beautify jewelry and various vessels. This art is a combination of fire and clay, blended with painting, creating beautiful designs. According to some experts, and through comparing Byzantine minakari with Iranian works, this art originated in Iran and then spread to other countries. However, ancient artifacts have been found in Europe with a much longer history. For example, six gold rings dating back to the 13th century BC were discovered in Cyprus, which are examples of enameled jewelry. Also, the famous statue of Zeus found in Greece dates back to the 5th century BC.
